Scientific References

Last updated: May 2026

Kegex pelvic floor exercise protocols and information are based on established clinical studies and peer-reviewed medical research. Below are the key scientific citations that support the methodology and recommendations provided within the app:

1. Dorey G, et al. "Pelvic floor exercises for erectile dysfunction."

Published in: BJU International, 2005.

This landmark randomized controlled trial investigated the efficacy of pelvic floor muscle training for men experiencing erectile dysfunction. The study showed significant improvement or resolution in pelvic floor strength and erectile function over a 3-month training course.

View on PubMed

2. Van Kampen M, et al. "Effect of pelvic-floor re-education on duration and degree of incontinence after radical prostatectomy."

Published in: The Lancet, 2000.

This randomized trial demonstrated that regular pelvic-floor muscle re-education significantly accelerates the recovery of continence and reduces the degree of postoperative urinary incontinence in men.

View on PubMed

3. Siegel AL. "Pelvic floor muscle training in males."

Published in: Urology, 2014.

This review article details the physiology, training protocols, and clinical applications of male pelvic floor muscle training (PFMT). It outlines the clinical effectiveness of training for post-prostatectomy incontinence, overactive bladder, and sexual dysfunction.

Scientific References

Last updated: May 2026

Tether's approach to task management, voice capture, and focus sessions is based on established clinical studies and psychological research on ADHD and executive dysfunction. Below are key scientific citations that support the methodology provided within the app:

1. Safren SA, et al. "Cognitive-behavioral therapy for ADHD in medication-treated adults with continued symptoms."

Published in: JAMA, 2010.

This study demonstrates that structured cognitive-behavioral interventions—specifically focusing on organization, planning, and distractibility—significantly reduce ADHD symptoms in adults. Tether's framework for capturing and organizing tasks is directly inspired by these CBT methodologies.

View on PubMed

2. Barkley RA. "Behavioral inhibition, sustained attention, and executive functions."

Published in: Psychological Bulletin, 1997.

Barkley's unified theory of ADHD highlights that the core deficit is behavioral inhibition, which impairs executive functions like working memory and self-regulation. By utilizing Voice Capture to immediately offload working memory, Tether helps bypass these common points of friction.

View on PubMed

3. Sonuga-Barke EJS, et al. "Nonpharmacological interventions for ADHD."

Published in: American Journal of Psychiatry, 2013.

This comprehensive review underscores the efficacy of behavioral interventions and environmental modifications in managing ADHD. Tether's Hyperfocus Mode creates a digital environment that minimizes external stimuli and promotes sustained attention.
